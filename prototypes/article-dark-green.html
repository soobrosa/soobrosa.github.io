Venturing down the IoT Rabbit Hole

6 September 2016 · soobrosa

The Microsoft Hackathon found us this year tinkering with some IoT-related technologies. Bernd, Bence, Balázs and me also tried to evaluate the reality of the commercially available IoT devices — and found a promising, but somewhat grim landscape.

We tried to bring to life four devices in different scenarios: the Samsung Artik 5, the WiPy, the Raspberry Pi 3 and the Sparkfun Blynk.

The Good

The smoothest learning curve was definitely provided by the WiPy. Connecting a sensor and typing the following lines in the prompt gives you instant gratification — and data.

> from machine import Pin, ADC
> import time
> adc = ADC()
> apin = adc.channel(pin='GP4')
> while True:
…     print(apin())
…     time.sleep(1)

We also verified that IoT cloud services are abundant and provide a good, free playground for the amateur to start out. The most promising emerging communication technology seems to be LoRa, as energy consumption goes down and range goes up.

The Bad

Getting serious is still hard given the immaturity of the ecosystems:

  • patchy documentation is everywhere, as most devices are still works-in-progress;
  • setup and discoverability is far from trivial, and default settings raise serious security concerns;
  • talking securely to remote services is hard — the OAuth dance is very outlandish to IoT;
  • there's no de-facto communication model, and developers aren't nudged toward best practices.
